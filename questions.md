## Context Setting
Establish what was already done before this A/B test. What are some questions you
can ask to get more information on what has already been measured, analyzed, or
attempted? List them below...

* Why do we want to do this?
* Is the company looking for an ideal word/slide count? What is the ideal that we're looking for? Do we have any assumptions for reading data?
* Why did we run this experiment and what was the hypothesis we started with?
* Did you purchase this data? How did you collect this data?
* We discovered that viewing time increased with lower word count. Is this an accurate representaiton of what you notice in real life?
* What are your goals? Why are you doing this?

Try not to ask yes/no questions.


## Goal Alignment
Align with stakeholders on what success means before interpreting any numbers.

* Which KPIs are actually important to you? Which KPIs are you responsible for? What does it look like when you achieve beyond your KPIs? 
* Define the KPIs with our stakeholders.
    * Start with intent -> KPIs
* How do you intend to use this data moving forward?
    * Non-profit --> funding

## Define Over-performance
Consider what it would mean if the treatment dramatically exceeded expectations.

* Which KPIs are explicitly not relevant to the project?
* What are the specific parameters of our intended analysis?
* What story are we trying to tell, and what does it look like when we've told too much?
* What questions are we not trying to answer, i.e. what specifically is beyond our scope?
* What is our protocol for identifying and coutneractong over-performance?


## Data Sourcing
Reflect on what other data sources you can use to answer your stakeholders
exploratory goals.

* Where does the data come from?
* Are the data from a trustworthy source?
* Are the data internally sourced within the company, or from an external source?
* Will the data be exessive in cost, i.e. be prohibitively expensive?
* Is the dataset publicly available?
* Is the data ethically sourced, i.e. with consent from participants, or a type of data that didn't requite explicit consent from a participating individual?
* Does the dataset contain PPI?
